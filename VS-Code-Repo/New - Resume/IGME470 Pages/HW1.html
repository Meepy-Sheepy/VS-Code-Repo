First Light

To make my first circuit, all I needed was an LED, resistor, switch, and a couple of jumper cables. All in all, it was pretty simple and figuring out how Power and Ground work together and the path that is required in order to make the LED light up is starting to make some sense. LEDs are polarized components, which means they only allow electricity to flow through them in one direction. There are 2 legs on the LED, an anode and cathode. Anode, the longer leg connects to Power while Cathode, the shorter leg, connects to Ground.

Series Circuits

After I did the first circuit, the book suggested I do Series Circuits. In this scenario, I would be adding another switch to the equation. Series mean that the same electrical current flows through both switches, meaning that they have to both be pressed in order for the LED to light up. I think of this as an AND statement in programming. A and B need to be TRUE in order for the LED to light up.

This was a pretty cool exercise as I further understood how connection and flow of electricity works. One thing that keeps tripping me up is how connected the wires are to the breadboard. I've had some connectivity issues and I'm hesistant to put pressure on this seemingly fragile object. I did SPEND 80 bucks on this thing!

Parallel Circuits

Finally, the last thing Chapter 1 wanted me to try was Parallel Circuits. This one is very similar to the Series one but instead of having to press both buttons in order for the LED to light up, you can press either one. This is very similar to an OR statement, where either A OR B can be true for the LED to light up.

This is where my understanding of connectivity finally solidified. Using a jumper cable, we connect the top most button to the LED and for the bottom button we keep the same jumper cable as it already connected to the LED. We essentially skip over the second connection with the switch, making the circuit a Parallel.

Conclusion

Overall, Arduino, as it stands, is a really good starter for people that never done anything with wires and electronics.
