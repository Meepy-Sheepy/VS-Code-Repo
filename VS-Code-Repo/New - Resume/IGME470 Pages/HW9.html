The Nitty Gritty

There's 2 new things other than the motor and battery I'm introduced to: Transistors and Diodes.

Transistors are components that allow you to control high current and high voltage power sources from the low current output of the Arduino. They're like digital switches in a way--like when you provide voltage to one of the pins known as a gate, it closes the circuit between the other 2 pins, called the source and drain. We can turn a higher current/voltage motor on and off with your Arduino.

I techncally know about diodes right now. LEDs are considered diodes, where currents can only go one way. However, this diode I'm using is to be able to prevent back-voltage coming from the motor when the motor is still running even when I do not press the button. I put it in the reverse of the circuit, that means, the negative (or cathode) connects the power of the motor while the positive (anode) connects to the ground of the motor.
