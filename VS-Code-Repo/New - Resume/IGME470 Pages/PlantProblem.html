My Plant Problem is going to target the moisture level of the plant, as to not over or under-water the plant. The parts I need consists of a moisture sensor combined with the LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) and a potentiometer. Overall quite the simple circuitry, but let's get dig in! (Get it?)

A Leaf of Faith

Now, what is a moisture sensor? Luckily, the name is what it is. It can be used to monitor soil moisture levels in real-time. The capacitance of the soil moisture sensor varies depending on the amount of moisture present in the soil. When the soil is dry, the capacitance of the sensor is low, while when the soil is wet, the capacitance is high. The sensor I have was a cheap $9 one on Amazon and it came in a 5 pack--a very unnecessary amount but why not right?

With those bought, I could've have the data from the sensor to output to the console in the Arduino IDE, but that's lame and minimalist, so I gave myself an excuse to use the LCD screen (You only use it once in the book!) to display text depending on the moisture of the soil. The potentiometer is just used to change the brightness of the LCD screen. The main reference for this project was Chapter 11: Crystal Ball. I would say that chapter is definitely in my top 3 favorites.

It may be hard to read in the GIF, but here's the different states the LCD can display--from Dry to Wet.

  1. "I CRAVE water."
  2. "My crave is sated. Thank."
  3. "You can stop now father."
  4. "I'M DROWNING!"

I wanted to add more text--adding father in all the dialogue options, but the LCD screen is 16x2, meaning I can only have 16 characters per row and a max of 2 rows.

Code

While the circuitry was simple, the code was also very simple. Since my project revolves around the modification of the Crystal Ball project, all I really needed was 2 variables:

  1. moisturePin - The pin where the moisture sensor is connected to.
  2. moistureVal - The value that the moisture sensor is outputting based on the moisture level.

In Loop(), I read the moisturePin's values and assign them to the moistureVal. The next thing is getting those values and printing out a state depending on the moisture level. This is where the if statements come in. When initially testing the values, it had this weird range from ~469 to ~234. I tried to map the values to a 0 to 235 (the difference of those 2 values), but it didn't work the way I wanted it to so I had to use the raw values.

After reseaching the plant I got for 5 bucks at a RIT vender, it was a jade plant. They are very easy to maintain and don't require watering until the top 2 inches of soil is completely dry but it can be a problem if they're overwatered. So I guessimated some values to align with the needs of this plant by having a low range of craving water, a big range of the sated state, and a mid-range of being overwatered.
